After a 31-year wait to reach the final of a European competition,\u00a0AS Roma<\/b>\u00a0have now done so in two consecutive seasons under the guidance of\u00a0Jos\u00e9 Mourinho<\/b>.<\/p>\n

In his first year in charge the enigmatic coach secured the club\u2019s\u00a0first European triumph since the 1961 Inter-Cities Fair Cup<\/b>, beating Feyenoord in the inaugural season of the Europa Conference League.<\/p>\n

Mourinho now leads the team into the\u00a0Europa League final against Sevilla\u00a0on Wednesday. The 60-year-old has won all five of the European finals in which his teams have played, but he insists that he is paying no attention to his impressive record.<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t think too much about what happened before,\u201d he said in an\u00a0interview with UEFA.\u00a0\u201cWhat happened, happened.\u201d<\/b><\/p>\n

\u201cThe difficult moments with [those] feelings, you also don\u2019t forget them, but you get used to living with them.\u00a0And I always look forward<\/b>. Maybe that\u2019s my secret or my philosophy, of being in football for so many years, so this final for me is a new final.\u201d<\/p>\n
